# Socratic Question Bank

Use these questions when the user's idea is too vague to critique directly. Ask only the questions that change the decision.

## Universal

- What decision are we trying to make after this conversation?
- What would count as success, and by when?
- What would make this obviously not worth doing?
- Which part are you most tempted to hand-wave?
- What have you already observed directly, not inferred?
- What must be true for this to work?
- Which assumption would kill the whole plan if false?
- What is the cheapest way to falsify that assumption?

## Requirements And Product

- Who is the exact user, and what do they currently do instead?
- What painful moment triggers the need?
- What output must the user trust?
- What edge case would make the product feel broken?
- What workflow should stay manual for the first version?
- Which feature is there to make you feel complete but does not prove demand?

## Technical Plan

- What existing invariant could this violate?
- What data or state migration is hidden in the phrase "just add"?
- Where will this fail silently?
- What would rollback require?
- Which part needs an automated test before implementation?
- Which dependency or abstraction are you adding before it is earned?

## Business, Market, Pricing

- Who pays, who uses, and who blocks adoption?
- What budget line does this come from?
- What current alternative is "good enough"?
- What channel can reach buyers repeatedly?
- What would a competitor copy in one week?
- What evidence proves willingness to pay rather than curiosity?

## Personal Decisions

- What are you hoping this decision will say about you?
- What are you afraid will be true if you do not do it?
- What quiet loss will not show up immediately?
- Who else pays the cost if you are wrong?
- What reversible experiment could you run before committing?
- What would five-years-later-you be angry that you ignored?
